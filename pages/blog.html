The Pivot
By Pau Joquino
January 19, 2016

YouthHack events are always something I look forward to because every event has its own defining learning experience. With the team retreat last week, "pivot" best describes my learning experience. Pivot is a term used in product development to refer to changing product features after testing the product and receiving feedback. It has recently become a buzzword used to refer to startups and businesses that change their scope or approach in order to survive or adapt to the market. At its most basic level, a pivot is all about change; it's a shift in perspective or position. During the day-long retreat designed to introduce the newly accepted members to YouthHack, I was also reintroduced to the community I had joined less than a year ago. As much as YouthHack has grown in the past year with all the programs implemented in YouthHack Manila, Philadelphia, Hong Kong, and Singapore, along with (YouthHack's head) David Ongchoco's email flooding with interested chapter founders based in Italy, Dubai, Nigeria, and Korea, this burgeoning community is still a toddler feeling the ground before it can resolutely stand on its own two feet. In Tagalog, "nangangapa pa." While this metaphor won't immediately inspire confidence, I believe it's YouthHack's greatest strength.
